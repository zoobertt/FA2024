Lirix

LIRIX’s website features a clean, minimalist design that effectively reflects its modern, professional brand. The strong use of neutral tones with bold accents creates visual appeal, while the intuitive navigation and clear typography enhance user experience. However, the design could benefit from more dynamic visual elements to avoid a monotonous feel. Additionally, some areas could improve in contrast for better accessibility, particularly for users with visual impairments. Overall, it’s a polished design, though small adjustments in visual variety and accessibility could make it even stronger.

Samurai

The design of this website is visually striking, particularly in how it invites interaction. The high-quality scans and images showcase textures in the paper, adding a tactile element to the experience. You can almost feel the surface of the materials as you explore the site, which gives it a unique, immersive quality. However, one area that could use some refinement is the "Legend and Mythology" section. The scrolling effect in this part, where images shift back and forth, creates an engaging sense of movement but can be distracting. The text overlaid on these images becomes hard to read as it gets lost in the motion, making the experience somewhat dizzying. A more subtle scrolling effect or clearer separation between the moving visuals and the text could improve readability without sacrificing the dynamic interaction.

Weedensenteret

This website is highly effective, not just because the colors, typography, and graphics harmonize, but also due to the engaging use of animation. The 3D designs are visually captivating and transition smoothly, adding an extra layer of interest. While it would be great to have the option to fully rotate them, watching them spin is still visually stimulating. The graphic design elements and 3D models work well both on their own and in combination, creating a seamless and dynamic experience.

Loreal

When I first opened the link, I was surprised by the aesthetic, as it didn’t immediately evoke the L’Oréal brand. While it clearly presents an image of innovation and a futuristic direction for the company, I wish the design had a bit more reference to their current brand style. The products being marketed feel more like alien weaponry than hair styling tools, which can be intimidating. That said, the website layout is engaging, giving the impression that the viewer is stepping into the future as they explore the content.

Resn

This website was a lot of fun to interact with. I explored the content multiple times—about eight, in fact—and each time it felt like a new, exciting interactive experience. The different styles kept things fresh, yet the black crystal element remained consistent, which I really appreciated. It seems like the website is meant to be experimental, showcasing various forms of interactive media that are each individually expressive.

Airpods

This website was definitely my favorite. It shares similarities with many of the other sites we've explored, featuring a variety of animations and displays that highlight the functionality of the AirPods. The design has a sleek, futuristic feel that complements the content without overwhelming it. The visuals are stunning yet keep the focus on the information, creating a balanced and engaging experience.

The Boat

The website for The Boat (SBS) offers a captivating and interactive storytelling experience, with stunning watercolor-style illustrations and seamless navigation. The design effectively draws users into the narrative, with each panel unfolding smoothly. However, the pacing can feel slow at times, and the interactive elements may not suit all readers. While the visuals are beautiful, the site could improve accessibility, such as better text contrast and more navigation options. Additionally, performance issues may arise on older devices or slower internet connections. Overall, it’s a unique and immersive experience, though a few adjustments could enhance usability and flow.
